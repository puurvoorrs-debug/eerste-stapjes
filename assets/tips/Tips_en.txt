Did you know... Newborn babies don't cry real tears for the first few weeks? Their tear ducts are still developing, so it's usually just sound.

Showing colors: Babies are born with a preference for contrasts. They see black-and-white patterns best in the first few months.

Did you know... A baby has twice as many taste buds as an adult? They are even located on their roof of the mouth and tonsils.

Mini-marathon: In the first year, a baby triples their birth weight on average. That is a real athletic achievement!

Growing in your sleep: Babies literally grow when they sleep. During deep sleep, their body produces human growth hormone.

Did you know... Newborns can only focus at a distance of about 20 to 30 centimeters? Exactly the distance between your face and the baby during feeding.

Strong hands: A newborn baby's grasp reflex is so strong that they could support their own body weight. (But don't try this!).

No kneecaps: Babies are born without hard kneecaps. This is still soft cartilage, which is very useful for later crawling.

Language skills: Babies can hear the difference between all languages in the world. Only around 6 to 12 months do they start focusing purely on the language you speak.

Did you know... Your baby's brain doubles in size during the first year?

💤 Sleep & Rest
Tip of the day: Do you notice your baby rubbing their eyes, pulling their ears, or looking away? These are the first signs of tiredness. Don't wait until the crying starts!

Better sleep: Put your baby to bed awake but sleepy. This is how they learn to fall asleep independently in their own familiar environment.

Routine is key: A fixed pre-sleep ritual (for example: bath, pajamas on, singing a song) gives your baby a sense of security and a signal that it is time to sleep.

Did you know... The 'Moro reflex' (the sudden jump with the arms) is very normal? Swaddling can help in the first few months to prevent your baby from waking up startled.

Day and night rhythm: Help your baby learn the difference by keeping the curtains open during the day and making normal noises, and keeping it dark and quiet at night.

🍽️ Nutrition & Feeding
First foods: Start around 4 to 6 months with the first practice bites when your baby is ready (watching your food, smacking lips). Begin with soft, mild flavors like carrots, cauliflower, or banana.

Patience is key: Sometimes a baby has to try a new taste 10 to 15 times before they like it. So don't give up too quickly!

Tip of the day: Never force a bite. If your baby keeps their mouth tightly closed or turns their head away, they've had enough for now.

Did you know... A newborn baby's stomach is as big as a cherry on day one? No wonder they want to drink a little bit so often.

Drinking water: Until your baby is 6 months old, they do not need extra water. Breast or bottle feeding is more than enough and contains all the fluids they need.

🧸 Play, Learn & Interaction
Tummy Time: Place your baby on their stomach regularly from birth when they are awake. This strengthens the neck and back muscles, which is essential for later rolling and sitting.

Peekaboo! Around 6 months, babies start to understand that something that is gone still exists (object permanence). Peekaboo is now the ultimate game.

Talking pays off: Talk to your baby a lot and name what you are doing ("Mom is putting your coat on now"). This is the absolute basis for their own language development.

Mirror image: Babies love faces. Hang a baby-safe mirror near the playpen; they will look at that 'other baby' fascinated for hours.

Tip of the day: Sing songs to your baby. The repetition, rhythm, and your familiar voice are super soothing and good for memory.

Discovering with the mouth: Does your baby put everything in their mouth? That's part of it! The mouth is currently more sensitive than the fingers and is the sense to discover the world.

🛠️ Practical Parent Hacks & Care
Handy onesie hack: Did you know that the envelope necklines of onesies are meant to pull the onesie down over the shoulders? Ideal in case of a blow-out diaper!

Skin-to-skin: Cuddling with your baby in just a diaper against your bare chest regulates your baby's heart rate, temperature, and breathing, and reduces stress (also for you!).

Teething? Put a teething ring in the fridge (not in the freezer!). The cold numbs the gums and soothes the pain.

Clipping nails: Clipping those tiny nails is easiest when your baby is deep asleep.

Deciphering crying: Pay attention to the reflexes: a 'Neh' sound often means hunger (due to the sucking reflex), while an 'Owh' sound often stands for tiredness (yawning).

Leak-free nights: Does your baby often leak through the diaper at night? Try using one diaper size larger for the night than during the day.

❤️ Emotional Development & Milestones
The first smile: Around 6 weeks you can expect the first 'social smile'. This is no longer a cramp, but a real reaction to your face!

Stranger anxiety: Around 8 to 9 months, your baby may suddenly start crying when you walk out of the room or if a stranger gets too close. This is a healthy milestone: they now realize you are unique.

Did you know... Babies can sense their parents' emotions flawlessly? If you have stress, your baby can become more restless. So take some time for yourself as well.

Self-image: Only around 18 months do babies discover that the baby in the mirror is their own reflection. Until then, they think it's a playmate!

Babbling: Around 4 months the pre-babbling starts. Answer when your baby makes sounds; this is how you teach them how a conversation works.

Discovering feet: Around 4 to 5 months, your baby discovers their own feet. It's a great workout for the abs when they try to put their toes in their mouth.

Crawling is custom work: Not every baby crawls on hands and knees. Belly-crawling, bottom-shuffling, or rolling backwards are also perfect ways to get from A to B.

Tip of the day: Enjoy the pace of your own baby. Every child develops in their own unique way and speed. Comparing is not necessary!
